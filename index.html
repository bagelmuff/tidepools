what is a tide pool?

A tide pool is a small pocket of seawater that gets left behind in the rocks when the tide pulls out. It rests there in the intertidal zone, the part of the shore that's underwater at high tide and exposed at low tide. It holds onto a bit of the ocean until the sea returns to fill it again.

biodiversity in tide pools :)

After a visit to Vancouver island, one of my favourite memories was looking at all the small creatures in the tide pools. Tide pools are incredibly biodiverse and have complex ecosystems. Fun Fact: Research in some rocky shores has identified 441 individual fish across 23 different species in a single tide pool sampling.
